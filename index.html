World Happiness Report

The focus of this visualization is exploring the World Happiness Report from Kaggle in the year 2019, which ranks 156 countries by their happiness levels. The report primarily uses data from Gallup World Poll. The rankings of national happiness are based on a Cantril ladder survey. Nationally representative samples of respondents are asked to think of a ladder, with the best possible life for them being a 10, and the worst possible life being a 0. They are then asked to rate their own current lives on that scale.

First, let's take an Overall Glance on the Happiness Score Around the World in the year 2019 before we dive deeper into our topic!

Overall Glance on Happiness Score Around the World

This Equal Earth map projection shows the happiness score around the world, with darker colour having a higher Happiness Score.

Most of the countries in Europe and America are overall happier than other countries in the world.

Main factors that contribute in evaluating the happiness in each country:

  • Score: A metric of Happiness Score (on scale of 0 - 10)
  • Economy GDP: A measure of how well a country is doing economically
  • Social Support: How much a country values friends, family and other people
  • Life Expectancy: The average number of years that a newborn can expect to live in "full health"
  • Freedom: How free an individual feels in the given country to make life choices and actions, unconstrained by external parties
  • Generosity: How generous the people are in the given country
  • Trust in Government: How much does the people in the given country trust their government

Correlation between these factors and Happiness Score

Economy GDP, Life Expectancy, and Social Support play the most significant role in contributing to happiness. Trust in Government and Generosity have the lowest impact on the happiness score.

Freedom correlates fairly with happiness score, but it does so with other factors as well, hence it does not really count as high impact to the happiness score in this case.

Another interesting point is, Economy, Life Expectancy and Social Support correlates well within each other too.

Does strong economy leads to healthier life and better social life?

The stronger the economy of a country, the better the social support as well as life expectancy.

Strong economy implies people of the country has better access to housing, education, and health services which increases life expectancy.

Besides, economy is an important foundation for better social network, as people has extra time and resource to socialize more.

How can these factors affect the happiness score of countries around the world?

Average value of each factor for each region

This heatmap shows why some countries rank higher than others.

From the observation we have above from correlation plot, countries with higher Economy GDP, Social Support and Life Expectancy will theoretically have a higher happiness score.

Distribution of Happiness Score across Different Regions

It seems that countries in Europe and America are overall happier, due to their stronger economy, better social support as well as life expectancy than other regions. Countries in Asia & Pasific and Middle East are slightly less happier as their life expectancy was slightly lower.

Relative Ranking of Countries by Happiness Score and Factors

We can see that most of the top 25% happiest country around the world has high Economy GDP, Social Support as well as Life Expectancy. This further proves that our findings throughout this visualization was quite accurate and persuasive.

However, we can also observe that there are some outliers in the plot, notice that in the Generosity column, Indonesia and Myanmar has outperformed every other countries in the world, but their happiness score ranking is not even included in the top 50% happiest country.

When we hover across the two points at Generosity column, we can see that even though Myanmar and Indonesia being exceptionally generous here, but low GDP and low-ish life expectancy hinder its claim to fame in terms of happiness.

To conclude, even though these factors might not have direct impact on each country's happiness score, but they do explain why some of the countries rank higher than other countries, especially Economy GDP, Social Support and Life Expectancy.
